    About Me

    Research Experience

    Doctoral Assistant & Researcher — EPFL, Heritage, Anthropology and Technologies Research Group | Lausanne, Switzerland (2020–2025)
    Designed and executed a 7-month independent ethnographic fieldwork project in Chongli, China, conducting 80+ in-depth semi-structured interviews with government officials, state-owned enterprise managers, resort operators, and local residents. Collaborated within an international, cross-disciplinary SNSF-funded research team ("Uses of Cultural Heritage at the Beijing Winter Olympic Games of 2022"), regularly communicating findings across geography, architecture, anthropology, and sociology.

    Research Assistant — Peking University Shenzhen Graduate School, School of Urban Planning and Design | Shenzhen, China (2018–2020)
    Conducted spatial data analysis on live music venues in Chinese cities using ArcGIS and statistical software. Contributed to empirical research on the socialist danwei system in Beijing, analyzing institutional change and spatial transformation. Participated in interdisciplinary urban planning consulting projects in Pingtan Comprehensive Experimental Area (Fujian) and Yichang Urban Master Plan (Hubei).

    Skills

    Qualitative research & ethnography: Fieldwork design, semi-structured interviews, participant observation, stakeholder mapping
    Text & archival analysis: Policy and planning document analysis, institutional analysis, discourse analysis
    Spatial & mixed methods: ArcGIS, spatial data processing, basic statistical analysis, data visualization
    Languages: Chinese (native), English (fluent), French (A2)
