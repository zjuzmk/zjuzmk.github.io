title: "Platform-Mediated Live Music and Urban Cultural Spaces"
---

**2018–2020** · Peking University Shenzhen Graduate School

## Overview

This project examines how digital platforms have reshaped live musical performance in China and, in turn, produced new forms of urban cultural space. Moving beyond conventional narratives of the "creative city," the research traces the concrete spatial practices through which platform-mediated music events reconfigure the use and meaning of urban venues.

## Research Questions

- How do digital platforms transform the organization, spatiality, and sociality of live music performance?
- What new urban cultural spaces emerge from platform-mediated practices?
- How do musicians, audiences, and venue operators navigate the platform economy?

## Methods

- Spatial data analysis of live music venues across Chinese cities using ArcGIS
- Mixed-methods approach combining quantitative spatial analysis with qualitative inquiry

## Key Findings

Platform-mediated live music in China has generated new social practices that blur distinctions between formal and informal cultural economies. These practices produce distinct spatial patterns in the city — temporary clusters, pop-up venues, and hybrid commercial-cultural spaces that challenge conventional cultural planning frameworks.
