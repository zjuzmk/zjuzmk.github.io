**2018–2020** · Peking University Shenzhen Graduate School

## Overview

The danwei (work-unit) compound was the basic spatial and social unit of urban China during the planned economy era. Since market reforms, these compounds have undergone profound transformation. This project traces how the redistribution of property rights has reshaped danwei spaces, institutions, and communities in Beijing.

## Research Questions

- How has property rights redistribution driven the spatial evolution of danwei compounds?
- What institutional mechanisms mediate between property rights changes and spatial outcomes?
- How do residents and work-units negotiate the transformation of inherited socialist spaces?

## Empirical Case

Field research in Beijing examined danwei compounds as microcosms of broader urban transformation in China. By tracing specific compounds' spatial and institutional trajectories, the research reveals how the legacy of the socialist planned economy continues to shape contemporary urban form.
