---
title: "Reconfiguring the Mountains: Chongli's Extended Urbanization"
---

**2020–2025** · PhD Dissertation · EPFL

## Overview

This project investigates how the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics catalyzed the rapid transformation of Chongli — a former poverty-stricken mountain county 200 km from Beijing — into China's first Olympic mountain city. The research sits at the intersection of mega-events, city-region development, and a new paradigm of urbanization in mountain peripheries.

Drawing on seven months of ethnographic fieldwork conducted between late 2021 and 2024, the study traces Chongli's urbanization through three interconnected dimensions: the political-economic configurations underpinning mountain development, the evolution of ski resorts as pivotal social infrastructure, and the extension of Olympic momentum into rural spaces.

## Research Questions

- What institutional, financial, and political mechanisms drive mountain urbanization in the context of a mega-event?
- How do ski resorts function as both consumption enclaves for urbanites and livelihood resources for local communities?
- How does Olympic-driven restructuring reconfigure rural economic networks, social relations, and residents' livelihoods?

## Methods

- 7 months of ethnographic fieldwork in Chongli (2021–2024)
- 80+ semi-structured interviews with government officials, SOE managers, resort operators, and local residents
- Longitudinal dataset of policy archives, planning documents, and investment materials

## Key Concepts

**State Developmental Urbanism** — a framework introduced in the thesis to characterize the dominant role of the Chinese state in shaping mountain urbanization through development and urban planning policies.

**Territorial Business Model** — the institutional, financial, and political arrangements that transform a territory into an investable asset, linking land rent production to Olympic-driven financialization.
