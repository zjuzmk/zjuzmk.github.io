# Cultural Heritage at the Beijing 2022 Winter Olympics

**2022–2024** · SNSF Research Project · EPFL

## Overview

Part of the SNSF-funded international research project "Uses of Cultural Heritage at the Beijing Winter Olympic Games of 2022," this research examines how China's first Winter Olympics mobilized cultural heritage — both industrial and intangible — for urban regeneration and nation-branding.

Working within a cross-disciplinary team spanning geography, architecture, anthropology, and sociology, the project explored the creative and sometimes contradictory ways heritage was defined, preserved, and repurposed in the service of the Olympic spectacle.

## Research Focus

- How does the Chinese state adopt a **state-led approach to industrial heritage**, distinct from Western market-driven or civil-society-led models?
- What role does heritage play in **capital accumulation and urban regeneration** in the context of mega-events?
- How are competing narratives of heritage — local, national, global — negotiated in Olympic-driven development?

## Key Findings

The Beijing 2022 case reveals a distinctive state-led model where industrial heritage preservation is instrumentalized simultaneously for capital accumulation, urban regeneration, and heritage preservation — three goals often in tension in other contexts.
