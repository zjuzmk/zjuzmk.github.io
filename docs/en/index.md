I am a researcher at the intersection of urban planning, urban studies, and human geography. My work examines the political economy of regional development, peri-urban and marginal areas, infrastructure-led spatial transformation, and the politics of space under state-led development.

I recently completed my PhD at the Heritage, Anthropology and Technologies Research Group, École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland. My dissertation, *Reconfiguring the Mountains: Mechanisms and Practices of Chongli's Extended Urbanization Driven by the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics*, explored how mega-events and state strategy reshape mountain regions through financialization, infrastructural development, and socio-spatial transformation.

**Research interests:** Regional political economy and spatial governance · Extended urbanization and marginal area development · Infrastructure and state-led regional transformation · Urban-rural integration and social change · Urban heritage and sustainable regeneration
