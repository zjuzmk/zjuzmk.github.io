# About Me

I am a researcher at the intersection of urban planning, urban studies, and human geography. My work examines the political economy of regional development, peri-urban and marginal areas, infrastructure-led spatial transformation, and the politics of space under state-led development.

I recently completed my PhD at the Heritage, Anthropology and Technologies Research Group, École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL), Switzerland. My dissertation, *Reconfiguring the Mountains: Mechanisms and Practices of Chongli's Extended Urbanization Driven by the 2022 Beijing Winter Olympics*, explored how mega-events and state strategy reshape mountain regions through financialization, infrastructural development, and socio-spatial transformation. The research was grounded in seven months of ethnographic fieldwork in Chongli, Hebei, involving over 80 in-depth interviews with government officials, state-owned enterprise managers, resort operators, and local residents.

My research interests sit at the intersection of regional political economy and spatial governance, extended urbanization and marginal area development, infrastructure and state-led regional transformation, urban-rural integration and social change, and urban heritage and sustainable regeneration. I work primarily with qualitative and ethnographic methods — fieldwork, semi-structured interviews, participant observation, and policy and discourse analysis — complemented by spatial analysis using ArcGIS.
